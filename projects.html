PROJECTS

Research shows that art allows us to explore our sense of self and connect us to our larger community.Art helps humanize and communicate ideas, feelings and traditions while transcending cultural and geographical boundaries. As a cultural tool, any art form helps humanize and actualize the emotions, grievances, and fears of those who may not have another place to voice concerns.We are passionate about any relevant global social causes such as environment, health , education, corporate etc. We aim to use art forms to raise awareness and explore solutions.

"Pratishruti- Promise to be there"- Fundraiser for Marginalized Artists in India During Pandemic.

Fundraiser event: Dhrupad Jhankar 2021; Nov 20, 2021: 5:00 pm - 8:00 pm, Issaquah Village Theatre

Tickets :

In a big, diverse country like India, round the year, various festivals create a space for all kinds of folk artists belonging to all Castes, Tribes, Classes, and other marginalized minority communities. Their art highlight common people's voices, cultures, and resistance. These artists' livelihood depends on their artistic means. Moreover, many people, such as musical instrument makers, dance costume tailors, jewelry-accessories makers, painters, etc., depend on these artists. Thus, the community is interrelated to many livelihoods.

The pandemic world stopped these festivals, which hits hard this entire unorganized artist community. However, even the virtual art world is opening up for privileged artists who can use improved technology like video conferencing, youtube, social media, etc., paired up with their communication skills. Generally, these marginal art communities do not have documented bios or access to the internet or social media to market their skills in a pandemic virtual world. Statistics were never collected for this sector to scale the issues. Moreover, some art forms in virtual performances cannot provide justice to live shows.

We created a project, "Pratisruti- A promise to be there," to raise funds for these marginalized artists in pandemic India. Sadhana's first fundraising event is jointly hosted with Seattle-based Dance academy, "Dance Tantra." Dance Tantra graciously agreed to raise funds through their Annual recital "Dhrupad Jhankaar 2021". This event is donating all proceeds to "Sumanasa Art Foundation" in India who took over this titanic task to raise funds for marginalized artists.

 





Inspirational Video Series


“The Lock-down”

Team “Sadhana” is currently working on the release of a inspirational video titled “Lock-down” based on a motivational poem written by Irish priest Brother Richard Hendrick. This includes re-creation of audio with poem recitation and singing by multi-cultural human population around the world, Creation of original visual arts, thematic & storytelling Dance moves all done in isolation during a challenging quarantine time period.


 

“I can't breathe”

In this video mix of Bharatantyam and contemporary dance and music collage we wanted to portray that long history of movement against police brutality. It is a collaboration between Seattle-based Indian Classical dancer Piyali Biswas and Boston-based black ballerina/contemporary dancer Jasmine Forrest.




About us

Sadhana is a 501-(c)(3) non-profit organization established in 2019 based in Seattle WA, USA.

Sadhana aims to explore and highlight ways in which various art-forms can be used to create social campaign and awareness, to explore and highlight issues that impact everyone and explore a common thread across diverse cultural forms around the globe. Art is truly a global language; it speaks to our need to express, reveal, heal, and transform. Sadhana aims to nurture and promote arts such as Dance, Music, Theatre, Photography, Creative Writing, Painting and Fashion to highlight and educate about issues relevant to all of us.
